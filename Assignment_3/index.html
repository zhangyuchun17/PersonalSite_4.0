LGBT+ Rights Index - Global Comparison

The data for this project is sourced from Our World in Data, which compiles global assessments of LGBT+ rights over time. The LGBT+ Rights Index measures the legal and social recognition of LGBT+ individuals in different countries. It ranges from -3.73 to 13, where higher values indicate more comprehensive legal protections and rights, while lower values signify greater legal restrictions or discrimination.

Research Methodology:

This study visualizes the LGBT+ Rights Index across different countries using three different classification methods:

  • Equal Interval Classification (Mercator Projection): Divides the data into equal-sized ranges, providing a uniform comparison across all countries.
  • Quantile Classification (Equal Earth Projection): Splits the data into groups with an equal number of countries, helping to highlight relative positioning.
  • Natural Breaks Classification (Winkel Tripel Projection): Uses clustering to group countries with similar legal environments, revealing distinct patterns.

To ensure clarity, countries with null index values are excluded from the maps. The dataset is color-coded, with negative values representing restrictive or punitive laws, neutral values indicating limited protections, and positive values reflecting extensive rights and legal recognition. This project aims to illustrate the global disparities in LGBT+ rights and the spatial trends in legal recognition.
